1 Introduction

The Modular Analysis of Vegetation Information System (MAVIS), originally developed by

1.1 Aims

MAVIS aims to…

  1. Assist in the assignment of a set of sample quadrats to the closest NVC communities.
  2. Assist in the identification of site properties.
  3. … This element of MAVIS is intended as an exploratory tool, not ….

1.2 Scope

MAVIS is designed to analyse vegetation survey data in the form of grouped or ungrouped quadrats, for a single site, with or without cover estimates.

2 Instructions and Methodology

2.1 Survey Data

2.1.1 Requirements

  • Each species value must match a species present in the accepted species list.
  • Each quadrat value must be unique, even if in a separate group.
  • Each year value must be a four digit integer.

Three methods for entering data into MAVIS are provided; manual, upload, and example.

The list of species available for entry is currently composed of:

  • The vascular plants present in Stace (2010), retrieved from BSBI (2014).
  • The bryophytes present in Blockeel et al. (2021).
  • The lichens, charophytes, and Algae present in the NVC floristic tables.

2.2 NVC Assignment

  1. An R implementation of the Modular Analysis of Vegetation Information System (MAVIS). This method uses the Czekanowski index (Bloom 1981) to quantify the similarity between a floristic table composed from survey data (with a minimum of five samples recommended) and the NVC Floristic Tables.

  2. An R implementation of the method outlined in

    1. This method calculates the Jaccard similarity between user-inputted sample quadrats and pseudo-quadrats. This approach is noted as improving the accuracy of fit relative to the Czekanowski index for sample quadrats with poor species richness (Tipping et al. 2013).

2.3 Habitat Correspondences

Habitat correspondence data is drawn from three sources:

  1. The Joint Nature Conservation Committee (JNCC) Spreadsheet of Habitat Correspondences (JNCC 2008).
  2. For UKHab is drawn from UKHab V1.1 (Butcher et al. 2020).
  3. For the National Plant Monitoring Scheme (NPMS) data is drawn from (Pescott et al. 2019)

2.5 Analysis Metrics

… uses the results of the NVC Assignment module to calculate the following metrics:

  • Cover-weighted Hill-Ellenberg ecological indicator values:
    • Nitrogen Score (N) (BSBI 2017c)
    • Moisture Score (F) (BSBI 2017d)
    • Reaction Score (R) (BSBI 2017a)
    • Salinity Score (S) (BSBI 2017b)
    • Light Score (L) (BSBI 2017e)

Data for bryophytes was taken from BRYOATT (Hill et al. 2007) and data for vascular plants was taken from.

2.6 Diversity

Metrics assessed:

  • Total Species Richness
  • Simpson’s Diversity Index (Simpson 1949).
  • Beta diversity (Koleff, Gaston, and Lennon 2003)
